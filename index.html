01 / Core

Big feat.

Two sources of truth. This recorded canon governs the look. The privacy-stack review governs every product claim — a strong line is not an approved line.

Message · working, not cleared
Proposed wedge

The phone is the athlete

Most cryptography is invisible. Client-side proving is the opposite — for twenty seconds your own device does something only a data-centre could do a year ago, and you watch it happen. We stage that as a sports-hero moment: effort, a countdown, a finish line, a medal you keep.

Working promise

Press once. Your phone proves it. You keep the medal.

A proof is generated on-device and submitted to Starknet Sepolia; the reward is a soulbound badge with no tradeable value. The feat is real; the medal is a keepsake, not a payout.

Release ruleA strong line is not an approved line. Check clearance before publishing — especially any proving-time or privacy claim.

      04 / Rules
      
Protect the signal.

      
These are the release decisions. Everything else is implementation detail.

    


    

      

        
Always

        
    
          
  • Describe what the phone visibly and provably did.
    • 
          
  • Say "flagship / newer phones for now" when talking hardware.
    • 
          
  • Keep the medal a keepsake — soulbound, no tradeable value.
    • 
          
  • Run the motif one way: tap → prove on-device → submit → medal.
    • 
          
  • Use the approved lockups and lettering at original proportions.
    • 
          
  • Label working copy and clearance status honestly.
    • 
        

      

      

        
Never

        
    
          
  • Publish phone proving times/specs without engineering sign-off.
    • 
          
  • Promise total anonymity or automatic regulatory compliance.
    • 
          
  • Offer tokens, prizes, or anything farmable for a proof.
    • 
          
  • Imply it runs on every phone, or hide the on-device step.
    • 
          
  • Retype, stretch, trace, or regenerate the lockups.
    • 
          
  • Add third-party trackers — the privacy app must be private.
    • 
        

      

    


    
Sources & status

    

      
Visual canon
retro-athletic
Palette, type roles, lettering, and the tap→prove→submit→medal motif, derived from the vintage sportswear reference set.

      
Narrative
working
Jul 16 meetings: proof-submission volume is the metric; feat framing; flagship-only for ~2–3 months; nothing farmable.

      
Proving numbers
binding veto
~15–29s figures are server-class engineering data (12-core / 46 GiB). No phone number in public copy without sign-off.

      
Privacy claims
SNF-gated
Selective-disclosure wording must be legal/SNF-approved. Public-witness proofs are delegatable; private stays on-device.

      
Public copy
mixed
One line cleared for use; every other variant still needs clearance before publishing.

    


    
Production specification

    

      
Signature motif
One lane, left to right: TAP → PROVE (on-device, ~20s theatre) → SUBMIT (Sepolia) → MEDAL. The on-device stage is the point — never skip or reorder it.

      
Proving theatre
Show the real stages — execution trace → constraints → wrap — as the loading animation. The wait is the feat, not dead time.

      
Grain & halftone
Subtle film grain and halftone dot belong on hero fields for the vintage-print feel. Keep interface copy crisp and legible.

      
Lettering & fonts
Custom lockups persist as artwork. Everything else uses self-hosted Anton / Archivo / Oswald / Yellowtail + a system mono.

      
Manual chrome
The cream paper surface belongs to this manual. It is a background field, not a production brand token to paint UI with.

      
Release
The asset makes one point, ends at the medal, and carries no uncleared claim. If in doubt about a line, it is not cleared.
